Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery


Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery, located in the coastal town of Hikkaduwa in Sri Lanka, is a popular conservation project dedicated to protecting and preserving sea turtles. The hatchery plays a crucial role in ensuring the survival of these endangered species by providing a safe environment for their eggs to hatch and nurturing the baby turtles until they are strong enough to be released into the ocean. The primary objective of the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery is to increase the turtle population in the area by combating threats such as habitat destruction, pollution, and illegal poaching. The facility actively collects sea turtle eggs from nearby beaches, where they are at risk of being destroyed or harvested for commercial purposes. Once the eggs are collected, they are carefully incubated in a controlled environment to enhance the chances of survival. Visitors to the hatchery can witness the hatching process, which is a truly captivating experience. The baby turtles are then cared for in special tanks, providing them with the necessary nutrition and protection until they are ready to be released into the ocean. The hatchery also conducts public awareness programs and educates visitors about the importance of sea turtle conservation, the threats they face, and how individuals can contribute to their protection. In addition to its conservation efforts, the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery also conducts public awareness programs and educates visitors about the importance of sea turtle conservation. It helps people understand the threats faced by these magnificent creatures and how they can contribute to their protection. Visitors can learn about the life cycle of turtles, their nesting habits, and the significance of preserving their natural habitats. By supporting the Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery, visitors not only get the opportunity to witness the incredible journey of sea turtles but also actively contribute to their conservation. The hatchery serves as a valuable center for education and advocacy, promoting the sustainable preservation of sea turtles in Sri Lanka.


THINGS YOU CAN DO IN HIKKADUWA


  • Beach Relaxation: Hikkaduwa is famous for its stunning beaches. Spend your days soaking up the sun, swimming in the turquoise waters, and lounging on the golden sands. Narigama Beach and Hikkaduwa Beach are particularly popular.
  • Snorkeling and Diving: Explore the vibrant underwater world of Hikkaduwa by snorkeling or diving. The coral reefs near Hikkaduwa are teeming with marine life, including colorful fish, sea turtles, and even shipwrecks. Several diving schools and operators offer snorkeling and diving excursions.
  • Glass Bottom Boat Rides: If you prefer to stay dry but still want to witness the beauty of the underwater world, take a glass bottom boat ride. These boat trips allow you to view the coral reefs and marine life through a glass bottom, providing a unique perspective.
  • Surfing: Hikkaduwa is a popular destination for surfing, particularly during the November to April season. Experienced surfers can catch some excellent waves, while beginners can take lessons from the local surf schools.
